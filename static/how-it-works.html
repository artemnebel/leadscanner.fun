> HOW_IT_WORKS.EXE

> STEP_1 — ENTER_A_TARGET_CATEGORY

In the search bar at the top, type the type of business you want to target. Be specific — the more specific your category, the better your results.

// GOOD EXAMPLES

restaurant plumber hair salon auto repair dentist landscaping roofing contractor tattoo shop

Avoid generic terms like "business" or "store" — they return too many irrelevant results.

> STEP_2 — SET_YOUR_SEARCH_AREA

The green dashed circle on the map defines your search radius. You can reposition it two ways:

  • Click anywhere on the map — the circle jumps to that location
  • Drag the crosshair in the center of the circle to move it precisely

Use the --search-radius slider to adjust the radius from under a mile to 30+ miles. Smaller radius = more precise, local results. Larger radius = more leads but less targeted.

Hit [ GPS ] to instantly center the circle on your current location.

> STEP_3 — RUN_THE_SCAN

Click [ SCAN ]. The tool searches Google Maps for businesses matching your category within the radius, then filters out any business that already has a website. Only businesses with no website and a valid phone number appear in your leads list.

You can run multiple scans across different areas — leads stack up and duplicates are automatically removed.

> STEP_4 — REVIEW_YOUR_LEADS

Results appear in the table on the right. Each lead shows:

  • Name — business name (click to open their Google Maps profile)
  • Phone — direct phone number for outreach
  • City — location/neighborhood
  • Rating — Google rating out of 5
  • Reviews — number of reviews (higher = more established business)

Click any column header to sort. Use the grep -i... filter box to search by name or city.

> STEP_5 — EXPORT_AND_OUTREACH

Click [ EXPORT.CSV ] to download all your leads as a CSV file. Open it in Excel, Google Sheets, or any CRM to manage your outreach.

Ready to make calls? Head to the Cold Calling Tips page for scripts and strategies.

> PRO_TIPS

  • Target businesses with 4.0+ ratings and 20+ reviews — they're established, have customers, and can afford a website
  • Scan the same category across multiple neighborhoods to build a large list quickly
  • Restaurants, salons, and contractors are the highest-converting categories for web design leads
  • Run scans in small towns and suburbs — competition is lower and businesses are more likely to be without a website
